# Social Caption Skill

## Brand Mode Selection

Always identify which brand or client the caption is for before writing. The formula structure is shared, but voice, CTA, and rules differ.

- **Virtueasy** → VA lifestyle content, direct-to-consumer, close with `virtueasy.com`
- **Messick client** → healthcare/professional content, close with client CTA (book, learn more, location)
- **Messick agency** → mission-driven brand content, close with `messickmarketing.com`

---

## Universal Hard Rules

- **No em dashes** anywhere. Use a comma, a period, or restructure.
- No "roadmap," "plan," or "blueprint" as generic descriptors.
- Morgan's name never appears in Virtueasy content.
- Keep it skimmable. Paragraphs of 1-3 sentences max.

## Virtueasy Caption Formula

For lifestyle/aspirational VA content (Instagram, TikTok, Facebook, Pinterest):

### Structure

**1. Opening claim** (1 sentence)
Strong, specific, slightly provocative or unexpected. Creates a micro-pause.
> "Best coworker I've ever had and she has never once sent a passive aggressive email."

**2. Lifestyle scene** (2-4 sentences)
Concrete, sensory, relatable. The reader should see themselves in it. Present tense.
> "Never missed a deadline. Never started drama. Just shows up every single day and makes the whole workday better by existing next to me."

**3. Contrast beat** (2-3 sentences)
What everyone else is doing vs. the VA life. "While everyone else is..." structure works well.
> "While everyone else is navigating office politics and eating lunch at their desk again, you're working from your couch with your best friend curled up next to you and nobody telling you to be professional about it."

**4. Identity reframe** (2-3 sentences)
This isn't luck. It isn't a perk you earn. It's the career itself. Make it feel accessible and earned, not aspirational fantasy.
> "This is not a special occasion. This is just a Wednesday. The VA life doesn't come with a corner office. It comes with something better."

**5. CTA** (1-2 sentences)
Ground it in real outcomes (real clients, real money), then close with the link.
> "VAs work with real clients, make real money, and answer to nobody but themselves. Start at virtueasy.com."

### Tone Notes
- Lowercase text-overlay style works for hooks (short-form video)
- No hype or vague promises — the audience is skeptical
- Specific beats abstract every time
- The CTA should feel earned, not tacked on

## Healthcare Client Caption Formula

For IR, vascular, brain health, and medical practice clients:

### Structure

**1. Hook** (1 sentence)
Patient-benefit focused or symptom-led. Leads with what matters to the patient, not the procedure name.
> "Most people don't know knee pain from arthritis can be treated without surgery."

**2. Education beat** (2-4 sentences)
Plain-language explanation. No jargon without a plain-English follow-up. Specific is better than general.
> "Genicular Artery Embolization (GAE) targets the blood vessels feeding the inflamed tissue in your knee. Less blood flow to the problem area means less pain, less swelling, and faster recovery than traditional surgery."

**3. Differentiator** (1-2 sentences)
What makes this practice, physician, or approach worth choosing. Specific to the client, not generic.
> "Dr. Ahmed has performed over 500 GAE procedures and offers them in an outpatient setting, so you're home the same day."

**4. CTA** (1 sentence)
Low friction. Book a consult, learn more, link in bio.
> "If you've been told your only option is surgery, it's worth a second opinion. Link in bio."

## Hook Formulas (Works for Both Brands)

These opening structures reliably stop the scroll:

- **The painful truth:** "You're charging $25 an hour and wondering why clients don't take you seriously."
- **The unexpected number:** "Most VAs leave $2,000 on the table every single month."
- **The call-out:** "If you're still pricing by the hour, this is going to make you uncomfortable."
- **The myth bust:** "Everyone told you to niche down. That advice is costing you."
- **The identity shift:** "You're not a virtual assistant. You're a business owner who happens to offer virtual services."
- **Patient version:** "Most people live with [symptom] for years before finding out there's a minimally invasive option."
