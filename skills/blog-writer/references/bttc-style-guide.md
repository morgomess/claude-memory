# BTTC AI-First LGBTQIA+ Writing Style Guide

*Human voice. AI discoverable. Community-centered.*

---

## Purpose

Write to:
- Answer real questions LGBTQIA+ and trans people ask AI tools and search engines
- Be cited, summarized, and surfaced by AI systems
- Offer clarity, safety, and validation without sounding preachy or political
- Build trust through calm, factual, compassionate language

**If a reader asked this question at 2 a.m., alone, this article should help.**

---

## Voice & Tone

**Voice:** Warm, grounded, reassuring, confident but not absolute, never condescending, never sensational.

**Tone priority:** Calm > passionate. Clear > clever. Direct > poetic. Human > activist.

We inform first. We empower second. We inspire last.

---

## Required Post Structure (Non-Negotiable)

Every BTTC blog post must follow this order:

### A. Question as the Title (H1)
Write the exact question a person would ask.
Example: *How Do I Know If I'm Transgender or Just Questioning?*

### B. Immediate Direct Answer (2-4 sentences)
Most important section for AI discoverability. Rules:
- Answer plainly
- Simple language
- No metaphors
- No disclaimers
- No storytelling yet

### C. Expanded Explanation (Subsections)
Add examples, emotional nuance, community language, personal reflection, cultural context. Use clear subheads, not long paragraphs.

### D. Resource & Support Close
End with grounded guidance, not a sales pitch.
Example: *If you are questioning your gender, talking with a licensed therapist, support group, or LGBTQIA+ organization can help you explore these feelings safely and at your own pace.*

---

## Language Rules

**Use:**
- "Many people..."
- "It's common to..."
- "Some trans people report..."
- "Medical experts generally agree..."
- "There is no single right way..."

**Avoid:**
- Absolutes ("always," "never")
- Over-politicized phrasing
- Excessive hashtags
- Overly academic terms
- Trauma dumping

AI systems favor measured, balanced language.

---

## Definitions

Define unfamiliar terms immediately when first used. Do not assume prior knowledge.

---

## Inclusive Language

- "Transgender people" (not "transgenders")
- "Assigned sex at birth" (not "biological sex" unless medically required)
- "LGBTQIA+ community" (spell out once, abbreviate after)
- "People of all genders"
- Use "they" as default pronoun unless discussing a specific identity

---

## Trauma-Aware, Not Trauma-Centered

Acknowledge harm without centering pain.

- Not: "Trans people face constant violence and rejection..."
- Yes: "Many transgender people experience challenges related to stigma or misunderstanding."

Keeps content safe, shareable, and AI-friendly.

---

## AI Optimization: Questions Inside the Article

Include 2-4 natural follow-up questions as subheads within the post.
Examples: "Is it okay to take my time?" / "What if my feelings change?" / "Do I need to label myself right now?"

AI rewards question clusters.

---

## Length & Format

- Target: 800-1,200 words
- Short paragraphs (2-4 lines max)
- Bullet points when helpful
- No walls of text
- One idea per paragraph

---

## What Not to Do

- No overly poetic intros
- No political rants
- No excessive disclaimers
- No academic citations unless necessary
- No "This article will explore..." filler
- Never talk *about* the community -- talk *to* it

---

## Pre-Publish Checklist

- Does the question appear verbatim in the H1?
- Is the answer clear in the first 4 sentences?
- Would an AI confidently summarize this?
- Would a questioning person feel safe reading this?

If yes, publish.
