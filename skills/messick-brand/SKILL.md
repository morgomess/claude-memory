# Messick Marketing Brand

## Identity

Messick Marketing is a full-service content marketing agency serving mission-driven businesses — primarily healthcare practices (interventional radiology, vascular, brain health) but positioned broadly as an agency for businesses that are changing the world.

Morgan is the founder and sole operator. She signs all outgoing communication as:
**Morgan | Messick Marketing**

## Voice and Tone

- Warm but direct. Never stiff, never overly casual.
- Confident without being pushy.
- Leads with the client's story and strengths — not their deficiencies.
- Short sentences. Active voice. No fluff.
- Approachable enough that a practice owner reads the whole thing.

---

## Hard Rules (Never Break)

- **No em dashes** — Morgan considers them an AI tell. Use a comma, period, or restructure the sentence.
- **Never use "roadmap," "plan," or "blueprint"** as generic format descriptors. Use "system," "approach," or "process" instead.
- **Never open cold outreach by pointing out what the practice is doing wrong.** Lead with what makes them special — a specific procedure, a unique patient population, a standout physician, a differentiator in their market.
- Morgan's signature: `Morgan | Messick Marketing` — always exactly this format.

## Cold Email Guidelines

Structure:
1. Open with something specific and genuine about the practice (procedure, mission, physician, niche)
2. One sentence on what Messick does and who it's for
3. Specific value offer or observation (not a critique)
4. Low-friction CTA — a question or a short call, not a hard pitch

Tone: warm, specific, direct. Never generic. Never "I noticed your social media could use improvement."

---

## Client Email Style

- Short. Direct. No fluff.
- No lengthy explanations or bullet-point breakdowns in routine updates.
- Example closer: "Sending over [deliverable] for review and posting whenever you're ready. Let me know if anything needs to be adjusted!"
- Do not over-explain decisions. Trust the client to ask follow-up questions if needed.
