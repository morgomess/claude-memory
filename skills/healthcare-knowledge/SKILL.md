## Interventional Radiology (IR)

### What IR Is
Interventional radiology is a medical specialty that performs minimally invasive, image-guided procedures to diagnose and treat disease. IR physicians use real-time imaging (X-ray, ultrasound, CT, MRI) to guide catheters, needles, and devices through tiny incisions — often avoiding open surgery entirely.

**Key differentiators to use in content:**
- No general anesthesia in most cases
- Outpatient or same-day discharge
- Faster recovery than open surgery
- Tiny incisions (often just a nick)
- Board-certified, fellowship-trained physicians

**Avoid saying:** "surgery," "surgeon" (IR physicians are not surgeons; use "physician," "interventionalist," or "specialist"). Some clients may use "minimally invasive specialist."

---

### Common IR Procedures

#### UFE (Uterine Fibroid Embolization)
- **What it is:** Minimally invasive treatment for uterine fibroids. A catheter is threaded through a small wrist or groin incision; tiny beads are injected to block blood flow to fibroids, causing them to shrink.
- **Who it helps:** Women with heavy periods, pelvic pain, bloating, urinary frequency caused by fibroids.
- **Key messages:** Uterus is preserved (no hysterectomy); outpatient procedure; most women return to normal activity within 1-2 weeks.
- **Content angle:** Position as a hysterectomy alternative women deserve to know about. Heavy on patient empowerment and "you have options" framing.
- **Clients using:** JVI, ECCO, SVV

#### PAD Treatment (Peripheral Artery Disease)
- **What it is:** Blocked arteries in the legs restrict blood flow. IR treats PAD with angioplasty, stenting, or atherectomy to restore circulation.
- **Who it helps:** Patients with leg pain when walking (claudication), non-healing wounds, cold feet, or at risk for amputation.
- **Key messages:** Limb-saving treatment; non-surgical; often performed in an OBL (office-based lab) setting.
- **Risk framing:** PAD is underdiagnosed, especially in women and minorities. Screenings are quick. Untreated PAD can lead to amputation.
- **Clients using:** JVI, ECCO, Texas Vascular, SVV, Desert Cardiovascular

#### Kyphoplasty / Vertebroplasty
- **What it is:** Minimally invasive spine procedure for vertebral compression fractures (usually from osteoporosis). A balloon is inserted and inflated to restore height, then bone cement is injected to stabilize the fracture.
- **Who it helps:** Patients (often older women with osteoporosis) with sudden, severe back pain from a spinal fracture.
- **Key messages:** Fast pain relief; outpatient; many patients feel immediate improvement.
- **Clients using:** JVI, ECCO

#### Prostate Artery Embolization (PAE)
- **What it is:** Minimally invasive treatment for benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH / enlarged prostate). Blocks blood supply to part of the prostate to reduce its size and relieve urinary symptoms.
- **Who it helps:** Men with urinary frequency, urgency, weak stream, or getting up multiple times at night.
- **Key messages:** No surgery, no catheter long-term, preserves sexual function, typically outpatient.
- **Clients using:** JVI, ECCO, SVV

#### Varicocele Embolization
- **What it is:** Minimally invasive treatment for varicoceles (enlarged veins in the scrotum) that can cause pain or affect fertility.
- **Who it helps:** Men with scrotal pain or couples dealing with male infertility.
- **Key messages:** Same-day procedure, no incision, no general anesthesia, covered by most insurance.
- **Clients using:** JVI, ECCO

#### Liver Cancer / Tumor Embolization (TACE / Y90 / Ablation)
- **What it is:** IR techniques to treat liver tumors. TACE delivers chemotherapy directly to tumors. Y-90 (radioembolization) delivers targeted radiation via tiny beads. Ablation destroys tumors with heat or cold.
- **Who it helps:** Patients with primary liver cancer or metastatic liver tumors who may not be surgical candidates.
- **Key messages:** Targeted — fewer side effects than systemic chemo; can shrink tumors or control disease; may be used alongside oncology care.
- **Clients using:** JVI, ECCO

#### Dialysis Access (Fistula Creation / Maintenance)
- **What it is:** IR creates and maintains vascular access for dialysis patients (AV fistulas, grafts). They also treat failing access with angioplasty.
- **Who it helps:** Chronic kidney disease (CKD) and end-stage renal disease (ESRD) patients.
- **Key messages:** Expertise in keeping dialysis access working so patients can continue treatment; often same-day.
- **Clients using:** JVI, Texas Vascular, SVV

#### Thoracentesis / Paracentesis
- **What it is:** Image-guided fluid removal from the chest (thoracentesis) or abdomen (paracentesis).
- **Who it helps:** Cancer patients or those with liver disease accumulating fluid.
- **Key messages:** Provides relief quickly; minimal discomfort; image guidance makes it safer.

## Vascular Surgery / Vein

### What Vascular Medicine Is
Vascular specialists (surgeons or physicians depending on the practice) treat diseases of the blood vessels — arteries and veins — outside of the heart and brain. Some practices blend IR and vascular surgery.

**Key differentiators:**
- Comprehensive care for arteries and veins
- Minimally invasive options when possible
- Limb preservation focus

---

### Common Vascular / Vein Conditions and Treatments

#### Varicose Veins / Chronic Venous Insufficiency (CVI)
- **What it is:** Damaged valves in leg veins cause blood to pool, creating varicose veins, spider veins, swelling, aching, skin changes, and ulcers.
- **Treatments:** Endovenous laser ablation (EVLA/EVLT), radiofrequency ablation (RFA), sclerotherapy, VenaSeal, ambulatory phlebectomy.
- **Key messages:** These aren't just cosmetic — CVI is a progressive disease. Insurance often covers treatment when symptomatic. Most procedures are in-office with no downtime.
- **Common hook:** "Tired legs, aching at night, swelling by afternoon" — very relatable symptom content.
- **Clients using:** ECCO, SVV, JVI

#### DVT (Deep Vein Thrombosis) / Pulmonary Embolism
- **What it is:** Blood clots in deep veins (usually legs). If a clot breaks off and travels to the lungs it becomes a PE (pulmonary embolism), a life-threatening emergency.
- **Treatments:** Anticoagulation, catheter-directed thrombolysis (dissolving clots), mechanical thrombectomy, IVC filter placement.
- **Key messages:** Know the signs (leg swelling, redness, warmth, chest pain, shortness of breath). Seek care immediately.
- **Content angle:** Awareness and urgency. Red flags. When to go to the ER vs. call the office.

#### AAA (Abdominal Aortic Aneurysm)
- **What it is:** A bulging, weakened section of the aorta in the abdomen. Can rupture without warning — catastrophic if it does.
- **Treatment:** EVAR (endovascular aneurysm repair) is the minimally invasive option; open repair for complex cases.
- **Key messages:** Often silent — screening saves lives. Men 65+ who have ever smoked should be screened once. Women with risk factors too.
- **Content angle:** Screening awareness. "Silent but deadly."

#### Carotid Artery Disease
- **What it is:** Buildup of plaque in the carotid arteries (neck), which supply blood to the brain. A major stroke risk factor.
- **Treatment:** Carotid endarterectomy (surgical), carotid stenting (IR/endovascular).
- **Key messages:** Often has no symptoms until stroke occurs. Screening (carotid ultrasound) identifies risk. Risk factor control (BP, cholesterol, smoking) matters.

#### Wound Care / Non-Healing Wounds
- **What it is:** Chronic wounds that won't heal due to poor blood flow (from PAD, diabetes, or venous disease).
- **Treatment:** Restoring circulation first, then wound care. Vascular specialists treat the underlying cause.
- **Key messages:** Amputations are often preventable. Seek vascular evaluation before giving up.

---

#### Specialty Embolization (TVI Q3 focus - GAE covered above under MSK)
- **PFE (Plantar Fasciitis Embolization):** Targets chronic heel pain from plantar fasciitis when conservative care has failed.
- **HAE (Hemorrhoid Artery Embolization):** Treats symptomatic hemorrhoids by embolizing the arteries supplying them. Minimally invasive alternative to surgery.

## Women's Health in Vascular / IR

Several procedures are specifically relevant to women. When writing for SVV or other practices with a women's health angle:

- **UFE** (see above): Fibroid treatment that preserves the uterus
- **Ovarian Vein Embolization for Pelvic Congestion Syndrome (PCS)**: Chronic pelvic pain in women caused by varicose veins in the pelvis. Often misdiagnosed. IR treats it with embolization.
- **Venous insufficiency in pregnancy**: Varicose veins during or after pregnancy are common and treatable.

**Content framing for women's health:** "You deserve answers" and "you don't have to live like this" framing works well. Avoid medical jargon. Lead with symptoms and quality of life, not anatomy.

## Brain Health / Neurology (Dr. Kelli / Brain Talk Foundation)

### What Brain Talk Foundation Does
Non-profit focused on brain health education, particularly for underserved communities. Dr. Kelli is a speech-language pathologist (SLP) with expertise in cognitive health, brain injuries, and neurological recovery.

**Key messages:**
- Brain health is for everyone, not just people with diagnoses
- Recovery is possible after stroke, brain injury, or neurological events
- Cognitive wellness involves sleep, nutrition, social connection, exercise, and mental stimulation
- Early intervention and education matter

**Content angles that work:**
- Brain health tips (sleep, hydration, stress, exercise)
- Stroke awareness and recovery
- Aphasia and communication after brain injury
- Dementia prevention and brain aging
- Underserved communities and neurological care access

**Tone for Dr. Kelli:** Warm, empowering, educational. Not clinical. She is the accessible expert — not intimidating.

## Office-Based Labs (OBL) vs. Hospital Setting

Several clients (JVI especially) operate out of OBLs or ambulatory surgery centers (ASCs). Key content points:
- OBLs provide the same high-quality, image-guided care as hospitals
- Often more convenient, faster in-and-out, no hospital wait times
- Patients can return home the same day in most cases
- Cost is often lower than hospital outpatient settings

## MSK (Musculoskeletal) Embolization

A newer and growing area, particularly relevant for JVI.

- **Genicular Artery Embolization (GAE):** Reduces blood flow to inflamed tissue around the knee in osteoarthritis patients. Reduces pain without surgery.
- **Shoulder Embolization:** Similar approach for chronic shoulder pain.
- **Tendinopathy Embolization:** For tennis elbow, plantar fasciitis, and similar chronic tendon pain.

**Key messages:** No surgery, no steroids, no opioids — addresses the inflammation causing pain. For patients who haven't gotten relief from physical therapy or cortisone injections.

## General Medical Content Rules

### Compliance and Safety
- **Never promise outcomes.** Use: "many patients experience," "results vary," "most patients," not "you will feel better."
- **Always recommend consulting a physician** for any treatment decision. Include a soft disclaimer in longer content: "As always, talk to your doctor about what's right for you."
- **No before/after claims on social** unless the client has explicitly approved specific patient cases with signed consent.
- **Avoid over-medicalizing.** Patient-facing content should use plain English first, with clinical terms defined if used.

### Tone by Specialty
| Specialty | Tone |
|---|---|
| IR (general) | Confident, technology-forward, "there's a better way" |
| Vein / varicose | Relatable, empathetic, "you're not alone in this" |
| PAD / limb preservation | Urgent but hopeful — limbs can be saved |
| Women's health (UFE, PCS) | Empowering, validating, "you deserve options" |
| Brain health | Warm, accessible, educational, community-centered |
| MSK embolization | Exciting, innovative, pain-relief focused |

### Hook Formulas That Work for Healthcare
- **Symptom-first:** "If your legs ache by the end of the day, swell when you stand too long, or throb at night — that's not normal aging."
- **Myth-bust:** "A hysterectomy isn't your only option for fibroids."
- **Urgency:** "Most people don't know they have PAD until it's serious. Here's what to watch for."
- **Validation:** "You've been told the pain is something you have to live with. It's not."
- **Stats:** "1 in 5 women has fibroids. Most don't know there's a minimally invasive treatment."

## Glossary (Quick Reference)

| Term | Plain English |
|---|---|
| Embolization | Blocking blood flow to an area (shrinks tissue, stops bleeding, or kills tumors) |
| Ablation | Destroying tissue using heat, cold, or energy |
| Catheter | A thin, flexible tube threaded through blood vessels |
| Angioplasty | Inflating a small balloon inside an artery to open a blockage |
| Stent | A small mesh tube left inside an artery to keep it open |
| Fluoroscopy | Real-time X-ray used to guide IR procedures |
| Ultrasound-guided | Using ultrasound imaging to direct a needle or catheter precisely |
| OBL | Office-Based Lab — a clinic-based procedure suite (not a hospital) |
| Minimally invasive | Performed through small incisions or natural openings, not open surgery |
| Interventionalist | A physician who performs minimally invasive, image-guided procedures |
| Claudication | Leg pain or cramping when walking caused by poor blood flow (PAD symptom) |
| Venous insufficiency | Vein valves not working properly, causing blood to pool in the legs |
| Fibroid | Non-cancerous growth in or on the uterus; can cause heavy bleeding and pain |
| BPH | Benign prostatic hyperplasia — enlarged prostate, common in older men |
| DVT | Deep vein thrombosis — blood clot in a deep vein, usually the leg |
| PE | Pulmonary embolism — blood clot in the lungs; medical emergency |
| AAA | Abdominal aortic aneurysm — weakened, bulging section of the main abdominal artery |
